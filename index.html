The Life and Legacy of Charles Darwin

Charles Darwin is perhaps the most influential figure in biological history. Born in Shrewsbury, Shropshire, he was the fifth of six children of wealthy society doctor and financier Robert Darwin and Susannah Darwin.

In December 1831, he joined the HMS Beagle as a gentleman naturalist. The expedition lasted five years and took him around the world, particularly the coast of South America and the Galápagos Islands. During this time, his observations of flora, fauna, and geology laid the groundwork for his future theories.

His most famous work, On the Origin of Species, published in 1859, introduced the scientific theory that populations evolve over the course of generations through a process of natural selection. It was controversial at the time but is now the foundation of all modern biological science.
